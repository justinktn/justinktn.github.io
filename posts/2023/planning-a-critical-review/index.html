BLOG #4: Planning a Critical Review

Posted on April 28, 2023  •  5 minutes  • 871 words

Film: Interstellar (2014)

I. Who is narrating or telling what happens in the work?

a. How is the narrator, speaker, or character revealed to readers?

The story was introduced to the audience through the characteristics of the actions of a character present in the movie, Interstellar. As the story unwraps over time, Cooper as a protagonist has been seen to introduce his relationship with other characters which is done by visual and auditory techniques to pass down the information to the viewers.

b. How do we come to know and understand this figure?

As humans, we possess a unique capability to comprehend and interpret complex anomalies that other natural or artificial beings cannot. By engaging with the emotions and feelings conveyed by the author, we can gain a deeper understanding of the intricate nuances of these anomalies, allowing us to appreciate the depth and complexity of the human experience in ways that other forms of intelligence cannot.

II. How did the film balance the scientific concepts with the emotional aspects of the story, and was this balance effective in engaging the audience?

a. Did it strike a good balance or did it lean too heavily towards one or the other?

Yes, the movie Interstellar strikes a good balance between scientific concepts and emotional aspects because it effectively demonstrates the scientific basis of the story while exploring natural human instincts. The film conveys the idea that love can transcend human limits, which is a central theme that adds emotional depth to the story.

b. Did the scientific elements detract from the emotional resonance of the story, or did the emotional elements undercut the scientific credibility of the film?

While the emotional elements in the movie Interstellar add depth to the story, they also undermine the scientific credibility of the film. For instance, the father’s decision to prioritize the time dilation effects caused by the theory of relativity over the love for his children makes it difficult to reconcile the emotional and scientific elements of the story. The fact that the characters experienced different timeframes on the planets they visited also challenges the scientific accuracy of the film. However, it’s important to note that the fictional nature of the movie limits its real-life applicability, and therefore, doesn’t necessarily undercut the emotional resonance of the story.

III. How did the film’s ending impact the overall impression of the story?

a. Did it feel like a satisfying conclusion to the narrative, or did it leave you with unanswered questions or unresolved plot points?

The movie Interstellar left a lasting impression on me by emphasizing the idea that love transcends time, as demonstrated by the gravitational anomaly depicted in the film. This is especially evident when the film explores the concept that time dilation causes time to pass at different rates depending on one’s proximity to Earth. By highlighting this scientific phenomenon, the movie underscores the power of love to overcome even the most fundamental physical laws, leaving a profound and thought-provoking message that resonates with viewers long after the movie has ended.

b. Did the ending feel like a logical extension of the themes and ideas explored throughout the film, or did it feel like a forced or contrived resolution?

Yes, the ending of Interstellar felt like a logical extension of the themes and ideas explored throughout the film. The movie establishes early on that love is a powerful force that transcends time and space, and this idea is reinforced throughout the story, particularly through the relationship between Cooper and his daughter, Murph. As the story progresses, the mysterious “ghost” that Murph has been seeing is revealed to be a manifestation of Cooper’s presence in the Tesseract, which he can enter due to the effects of the black hole. The resolution of the film ultimately relies on a combination of scientific and emotional elements, which are intricately woven together to create a powerful and moving conclusion. Through the character of Murph, the audience can see the culmination of Cooper’s efforts, as she uses the data he collected while inside the Tesseract to solve the equation that will allow humanity to leave Earth and colonize other worlds. Therefore, the film’s ending feels like a natural and organic extension of the themes and ideas explored throughout the narrative, providing a satisfying and emotionally resonant conclusion to the story.

Brief Reflection:

I am amazed by the complexity of the film’s narrative and the depth of the ideas it explores. While some films can be enjoyed on a surface level, Interstellar requires a deeper level of engagement and understanding to fully appreciate. In particular, the film’s ending, with its focus on the bootstrap paradox and the interplay between time and love, is a particularly challenging but rewarding element of the story. I have come to appreciate the importance of sharing my insights with others. By helping others to understand the intricacies of the plot, the themes, and the ideas explored in the movie, I hope to contribute to a deeper appreciation and understanding of this complex and thought-provoking film. In conclusion, I believe that Interstellar is a movie that rewards careful attention and reflection, and I am grateful for the opportunity to engage with it on a deeper level.
