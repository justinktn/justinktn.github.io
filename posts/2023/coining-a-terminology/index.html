BLOG #4: Coining a Terminology

Posted on March 10, 2023  •  3 minutes  • 631 words

The term “Visionfactotum” combines the words “vision” and “factotum” to create a new word that refers to the ability to quickly adapt to changes in one’s environment or circumstances that are related to vision. It suggests that the ability to see clearly and adapt to changes in what we see is essential to success in today’s fast-paced world.

1. Which factors led you to coming up with this term?

The term “Visionfactotum” was inspired by the observation that in today’s fast-paced world, it is increasingly important for individuals and society as a whole to be able to quickly adapt to changes. For instance, the COVID-19 pandemic has forced us to adopt new habits and precautions, such as wearing face masks to prevent the spread of the virus, washing our hands regularly to eliminate harmful pathogens, and avoiding touching our faces. These changes require us to be more mindful of our environment and to develop new habits and skills to keep ourselves and others safe.

By combining the words “vision” and “factotum,” “visionfactotum” encapsulates the idea that adapting to changes related to vision is essential for success in today’s world. It suggests that being able to see clearly and interpret visual cues accurately is a foundational skill that underlies many aspects of modern life, from technology to business to interpersonal communication. As such, cultivating a strong “visionfactotum” is crucial for anyone who wishes to thrive in the face of constant change and uncertainty.

2. Why did you choose the words you chose for the term?

The term “Visionfactotum” was carefully chosen to convey the idea that adapting to changes related to vision is critical for success in today’s world. The word “vision” represents our ability to see and interpret the world around us, which is essential for making informed decisions and taking appropriate actions. In the context of the COVID-19 pandemic, for example, our vision has been a key factor in our ability to adapt and stay safe. We have had to adjust quickly to new information about the virus and respond accordingly, such as by wearing masks, social distancing, and practicing good hygiene.

The word “factotum” was chosen because it suggests the idea of a place where skills and abilities are created or produced. The case of “Visionfactotum,” refers to the diverse responsibilities that individuals have in today’s world. We are not only responsible for ourselves but also for our families and communities, and we must be able to adapt to changes in our environment and circumstances to meet these responsibilities effectively. This requires a broad set of skills and abilities related to vision, such as the ability to interpret visual cues accurately, make quick decisions, and communicate effectively.

3. How do you formally define your coined terminology?

The term “Visionfactotum” refers to an individual’s ability to quickly adapt to changes based on what they can see in their visual environment. It is a term that reflects the importance of visual perception and interpretation in our modern world, where we are constantly bombarded with new information and stimuli.

At its core, “Visionfactotum” is about being able to respond quickly and effectively to changes in our environment. This requires not only strong visual acuity but also the ability to interpret and process visual cues accurately and efficiently. Whether we are dealing with a pandemic, changes in technology, or shifting social norms, our ability to adapt and respond to these changes depends on our “Visionfactotum.”

Furthermore, “Visionfactotum” suggests that the ability to adapt and respond to changes is not only important for personal success but also for the well-being of our communities and society as a whole. By cultivating a strong “Visionfactotum,” individuals can play an active role in shaping the world around them and responding to the challenges and opportunities that arise.
